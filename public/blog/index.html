well, well, well, if it isn't the joys of living


I'M BACK AGAIN & better than before i fear. while i continue to experience the epic highs and lows of being alive and in the world i do think the highs are beginning to expand. tomorrow i am doing one little thing for myself & yesterday i did one little thing for this site (and also kind of myself) which was just spend a couple hours rearranging my software & debugging git & researching frontend development as if i did not kind of soft launch my budding career as Not a Frontend Developer months upon months ago, and that's been really fun.


i alsooo have been talking to a lot of people and making a lot of new friends and while i come into these relationships now with a sort of tentative knowledge that they might not last long or forever or even past this summer i think it's still beautiful. i'm really glad to have met them, despite the ephemerality of it all.


sun 1.11.2026

what was it that the mountain goats said?


it's the new year! well. at least for the western world. mentally i am waiting until april (cambodian new year), where i can finally relax the shoulders and sigh with the knowledge that new year's celebrations are over at least for another eight months, but also physically i think i'm ready for the change--i've been cleaning my room, reorganizing, finally getting things done that i'd promised to do last year. there's a peculiar blandness about it.


yesterday i got my first taste of true solitude since the beginning of november, and i took to it so quick i'm surprised i haven't again tried to take the screen off my window to bother the pigeons (i still don't really know how). i missed it. i'm sad i missed it, but i don't think i feel guilty about it anymore... it bleeds away with the evangelism that has stained my life for so long.


a year in review: i found out the rapture wasn't real and i spent the whole year feeling joy and immense grief about it in turn. people kept on dying and i kept on giving any amount of anything i came into possession of to them. there is never enough time and never enough ultimatums and never enough conversations to make the world feel less like it was fracturing at its roots. everything still feels a little hopeless right now, too.


yesterday, when i was alone, i wept over the second song i got to sing to myself. it was something about begging for someone to tell me the world is still beautiful. maybe that's my new year's resolution (can you still make those 11 days into the year?)--rediscovering how beautiful it is here. even as it bleeds.

sun 12.28.2025

somehow, the year isn't over yet


i keep finding myself glancing at calendars, waiting for the day that those last four numbers tick up one. it feels like this year's been way too short; it feels like it's been far too long. i've been sitting here, spending most of my time trying to recover from a hectic holiday season and probably one of the most recent worst couple of months of my life.


ridiculously, there's still enough in me to be hopeful. i think i'm building my way back now--realized that i'd fallen into a pit of somehow wholly new habits, still equally bad as last year's, and there's only one way to go after hitting rock bottom (again). it's definitely been a better year than last, but i still keep on pressing into it, like fingers on a thin film, searching for where it all breaks and the world or God or whoever's been watching all this go down says yeah, you fucked up beyond repair. no, there's no time left for you. but somehow the world keeps on turning and godawful movies keep on coming out and i get to watch them with my friends and isn't that something?


anywho. there's a couple more books i want to read before 2026 and a few silly resolutions to do and calls to make to really sweet friends. i'm glad the year is over, but i'm also, mostly, kind of, still glad i got to live it.
